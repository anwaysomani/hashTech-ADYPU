Team Description
A team may consist of a maximum of four participants, Students from different institutes can form a team.
Rules:
  1. The competition will be held in two rounds.
  2. The first round(Round-1) will be the elimination round.
    • The number of eliminations during a round will depend upon the number of entries.
    • The robot has to complete the full arena in minimum time.
    • In Round-1, you can skip a task with penalty of 1.5 minutes.
    • No points will be awarded for that particular checkpoint that is skipped.
    • The final list of teams that qualify for the next round would be taken after tallying the points gained and time taken to complete the track.
  3. The next round(Round-2) will be the final round.
    • In this round, the track would be changed. And this new track would be dislosed on the day of the competition.
    • The level of difficulty in this track would be more.
    • In round 2, you can skip a task with penalty of 2 minutes.
    • The main aim here is to gain maximum points by overcoming the obstacles.
    • The final decision of the winner would be taken after tallying the points gained and time taken to complete the track.
  4. If the bot goes out of the arena/track, a 10 second penalty will be awarded and the bot will have to start over the previous checkpoint.
  5. During the rounds, only maximum of two team members are allowed in the arena.
  6. Teams have to be ready with their bots. No time will be given once the robot is on the Arena. If the bot is proved to be inactive for more than 60 secs, it is disqualified.
  7. The Organizers have the right to Disqualify any participation indulging in malpractices. The decision of the Event Organizers is final and cannot be debated on.
Robot Specification
  1. The bot must fit into a cube of 300mm X 300mm X 300mm(lxbxh) and must weigh less than or equal to 3 kg(maximum error of 200gm will be accepted).
  2. The bot can be controlled wired or wirelessly.
  3. The battery used cannot exceed 12 V with max load current of 4 amp. The power supply may or may not be onboard. Participants should bring their own power supply. No bench supply would be provided by the organizers.
  4. If the robot of the participants is not working on the spot, they can use the Organizerâ€™s Robot.
  5. The robot must not leave behind any of its parts during the run, else it will result in disqualification.
  6. The robot should not damage the arena. It can lead to disqualification and fine of certain amount of money.
Certification Policy
  1. Certificate of appreciation/participation will be awarded to all teams.
  2. Certificate of excellence and momentous will be given to top three teams.
  3. Disqualified team will not be considered for any certificates.
